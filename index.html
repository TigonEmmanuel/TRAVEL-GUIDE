Accra

Introduction

Accra, the capital of Ghana, is a vibrant coastal city known for its rich culture, historic landmarks, and friendly locals. With a mix of traditional markets and modern infrastructure, Accra offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore West African heritage and hospitality.

Top Attractions

  • Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum

    This is the final resting place of Ghana’s first president and a key figure in Africa’s independence movement. The museum offers historical insight and beautiful gardens.

  • Independence Arch

    Located at Independence Square, this monument symbolizes Ghana's freedom from colonial rule and is a popular spot for photos and national events.

  • Makola Market

    A bustling local market where you can find everything from fresh produce to handmade fabrics and crafts—perfect for a taste of everyday Accra life.

  • Jamestown Lighthouse

    One of the oldest districts in Accra, Jamestown features colonial architecture, street art, and the iconic lighthouse overlooking the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Labadi Beach

    A lively beach known for its horseback rides, drumming, dancing, and weekend parties, making it a hotspot for both tourists and locals.

Foods to Try

  • Waakye

    A popular breakfast dish made from rice and beans, usually served with boiled eggs, spaghetti, and fried plantains. It's filling and full of flavor.

  • Banku and Tilapia

    A fermented corn and cassava dough served with grilled tilapia and spicy pepper sauce. This is a favorite among locals and offers a truly Ghanaian taste.

  • Kelewele

    Spicy fried plantains seasoned with ginger, garlic, and pepper. A great street snack that’s sweet, spicy, and crunchy.

  • Jollof Rice

    A well-known West African rice dish cooked in tomato sauce with spices. Often served with chicken, beef, or fish, it's a party and home favorite.

  • Kenkey and Fish

    Fermented corn dough wrapped in corn husk and served with fried fish, pepper sauce, and sometimes shito (black pepper sauce). A coastal delight!

Getting Around

Getting around Accra is easy with multiple transport options. Shared minibuses called "trotros" are the most affordable way to travel locally. Taxis and ride-hailing services like Bolt and Yango are also widely available. For longer distances or more comfort, air-conditioned buses and car rentals are good choices.
